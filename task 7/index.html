About BMW

Bayerische Motoren Werke Aktiengesellschaft (BMW AG), commonly known as BMW, is a German multinational conglomerate and a leading global manufacturer of luxury vehicles and motorcycles. Headquartered in Munich, Germany, the company operates under the brands BMW, MINI, Rolls-Royce, and BMW Motorrad. Founded in 1916 as an aircraft engine manufacturer, BMW transitioned to motorcycles in the 1920s and entered the automobile market in 1928 following the acquisition of Fahrzeugfabrik Eisenach.

The company is renowned for its focus on performance, engineering precision, and driver engagement, often marketed under the slogan "The Ultimate Driving Machine." In recent years, BMW has expanded its portfolio to include electric and hybrid vehicles, such as the i3 and ActiveHybrid models, while maintaining its heritage in motorsports through its BMW M division. As of 2025, BMW reported revenues of €133.453 billion and produced over 2.4 million cars and 200,000 motorcycles.

Key corporate details include:
Ownership: The Quandt family holds a significant stake, with Stefan Quandt owning 27.7% and Susanne Klatten owning 22.5%. Leadership: Milan Nedeljković serves as the chairman of the management board, succeeding Oliver Zipse in May 2026. Global Reach: BMW operates over 30 production facilities across 14 countries and is the ninth-largest producer of motor vehicles worldwide. History: The brand survived near-bankruptcy in 1959 after an investment by the Quandt family, leading to the development of the successful New Class sedans that defined its modern identity.
